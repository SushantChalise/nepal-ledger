## Draft text

**Money In.** Nepal's banking system absorbed another year of deposit growth in FY 2081/82 — total deposits reached NPR 7.30 trillion in Ashadh 2082 (+56.5% over 47 months), and the deposit-to-GDP ratio rose 10.21 pp to 119.59% [`Asar_2082_Publish.json`, C5/C4]. Savings deposits gained 2.84 pp of share at the expense of fixed (now 36.60% vs. 48.02%) — households are choosing on-demand over locked-up term, a posture of caution, not of confidence [`Asar_2082_Publish.json`, C4 deposit-composition ratios]. The deposit-account base reached 59.88 million, +58.5% over the window, while the branch network grew only 8.4% — Nepal's financial inclusion gain is now overwhelmingly digital. **Money Out.** Bank credit to the private sector grew only 32.5% over the same period and FELL 7.40 pp as a share of GDP to 91.60% — the banking channel deleveraged Nepal's real economy even as savings poured in [`Asar_2082_Publish.json`, C4 `--total-credit-gdp`]. The credit that DID flow re-routed: construction credit collapsed 44.5% in absolute NPR terms and lost 5.74 pp of share, while consumption loans tripled to 20.60% of the book and hydropower-aligned electricity credit gained 2.98 pp [C7 sector cuts]. Agriculture's share stood flat at 6.37% — the inclusion mandate is losing ground (deprived-sector loan share 8.25% → 5.52%) [C4 `--deprived-sector-loan-total-loan`]. **Money Captured.** Bank investments rose 137.4% to NPR 1.74 trillion, with government-securities holdings alone reaching NPR 1.17 trillion (+62.1%) — the State, not the productive private sector, was the marginal borrower of Nepal's net new deposit inflow [`Asar_2082_Publish.json`, C5 `investments` and `investments--govt-securities`]. Bank-class concentration drifted slightly toward commercial banks (HHI 7,914 → 8,100 on deposits); per-bank HHI awaits parser v0.2.0. **Money Wasted.** System non-performing loans climbed from 1.48% to 4.62% (peaking 4.92% in Poush 2081), with finance companies at 11.05% — 2.5× the commercial-bank rate and the canary of the BFI tier [`Bhadau_2082_Publish.json`, C4]. Retained earnings flipped from +NPR 75 billion to –NPR 31 billion across the window even as paid-up capital rose +NPR 78 billion — the banking system recapitalized itself out of shareholder dilution, not profit. Total capital adequacy fell to 12.95% (from 14.19%) at the system level; finance-class adequacy fell to 13.93% from 22.04% [C4]. **Where Money Becomes Wealth.** Productive-sector credit (agriculture + hydropower + manufacturing + transport + mining + tourism) gained 6.29 pp to 21.95% of total — but the gain is concentrated in hydropower; agriculture and manufacturing share is flat-to-down, transport is shrinking. The real-estate cluster held flat at 12.82%, so the real-estate-vs-productive ratio improved from 0.79 to 0.58 [`re_vs_productive.json`]. The system is becoming more productive in composition but less productive in scale — fewer rupees as a share of GDP are reaching the businesses that build wealth, even as the rupees that DO reach them are slightly better-aimed than four years ago.
